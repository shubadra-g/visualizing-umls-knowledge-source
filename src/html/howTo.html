UMLS METATHESAURUS

Visual Browser

 About | How To




UMLS Metathesaurus Visual Browser depicts concept relations for terms present in the Metathesaurus in the form of a radial layout. Below are a few ways to use the browser along with browser features.

Features of the Browser
  • The browser supports a list of terms and their concept relations. (List of Terms Available in the Browser)
  • Users can keep track of the searched concept as well as the clicked concepts during their exploration with breadcrumbs. These breadcrumbs populate with each click.
  • Data about the concept relations are present for three levels - searched term, its concept relations (level 1), level 1's concept relations (level 2) and level 2's concept relations.
  • The concept range slider focuses on concepts having outgoing concept relations within the selected range. When a term is clicked, the concept slider needs to be re-adjusted to filter for the clicked term.
  • Data on Demand: Hovering over a concept or a term provides additional information such as the CUI, Number of Concept Relations and a definition for the concept (if available).
How does the browser work?
  • Select search type - i.e., using the medical terminology name or CUI.
  • Enter corresponding text or ID in the search box. Please note: the names are case-sensitive. Hit enter or click the search button to begin search.
  • Once the term and its concept relations are displayed - the slider under the search can be modified to show relations between the range selected.
  • Hover on a concept to see more details, click on the concept to see its connected terms.
  • To search for a new term, start over!
